Frequently Ask Questions

What is [wpv-bloginfo]?

A new way for people to generate a collaboration is through the concept of internet crowdsourcing. The concept is fairly simple. [wpv-bloginfo] is the first internet platform that allows people to create, pitch and market their idea for a collaboration across the internet. Others on the internet can fund the collaboration with certain dollar amount donations. When the YouTubers involved in this campaign decide to collaborate with each other 75% of all donations and proceeds will go towards them. The collaborators decide what to do with the money.

[wpv-bloginfo] is the only platform where campaigners and supporters are partnering with us to build a world community of ingenious collaborations. Campaign creators and contributors are working closely together to make new collaborations come together in real life.

What do I need to know before choosing a campaign?

When looking at a campaign, one must view it through a philanthropist’s eyes. Contributing to a campaign is more like giving somebody an assignment to complete rather than acquiring an item from the store or trading stocks. When one supports a campaign, one supports an idea and a cause he or she equally has an interest in.

However, before someone makes a contribution to a campaign, we at [wpv-bloginfo] recommend to read these suggestions first.

  1. Ensure the person who created the campaign is offering full description with lots of information about the collaboration
  2. Make sure you can identify yourself with the collaboration
  3. Search and see if you can find more information about the collaboration.
  4. When you decide to enable [wpv-bloginfo] Smile Mode, select a cause or organization on the checkout page that you can identify yourself with. Read more about [wpv-bloginfo] Smile so you know your money is in good hands and will have a great impact

For many contributors, the rewarding part comes in the form of watching the campaign, from the idea to making the collaboration happen in real life.

One must also acknowledge the risks involved in such an endeavor. In preliminary stages, unanticipated delays and challenges can arise, and there is always the additional risk the collaboration will be unsuccessful.

How are contributors protected?

A level of security is necessary and needed by contributors before they start supporting a campaign. Contributors need to feel safe and secure that their money is going to an honest campaign, and there is validation behind their donations. [wpv-bloginfo] provides the trusted platform which contributors are seeking in crowdsourcing a collaboration.

Trust & Safety

[wpv-bloginfo] mission is to provide a safe and trusted platform for contributors to choose a campaign to support on a crowdsourcing sight; while simultaneously uniting people globally around ideas and collaborations that are important to them and then empowering those people to see those collaborations become a reality.

Watching every campaign grow from an idea to an extraordinary collaboration, we want every contributor to be well informed. [wpv-bloginfo] provides all the information and a global community built on trust and passion for seeing “dream collaborations come true”, and the security needed for the donors to feel confident supporting their favorite campaign.
Frequently, we may get asked certain questions about the viability of certain campaigns. First, we encourage everybody to evaluate the campaign on their own. We want contributors to be as informed as possible; learn about the campaign by reading more about the suggested collaborators, their biographies, updates, and comments.

Ask yourself is this a collaboration I can relate to or get excited about? We encourage everybody to be involved in the discussion/in the comments. As part of a global community, if one has a question, it is probably a question somebody else has and should be added to the campaigns updates/comments.

What is the role of the contributor?

[wpv-bloginfo] and all campaigns could not be successful without the unbelievable aid of our community of donors. Therefore, we are dedicated to securing donors with the information, tools and safety needed to determine a donation to a campaign. We realize our contributors have the ability to make a new collaboration happen each time they support a campaign. We agree that this can be filled with both risks and rewards, but we want our contributors feeling confident and excited on the journey. The key is trust between all three parties: [wpv-bloginfo], the campaign creators, and the donor.
It’s with trust and support that great collaborations come to life.

What status can a campaign have?

The status in the Campaign Stage can be described as follows:


  • Draft Status: When creating a campaign creators have the possibility to save their campaign as a draft in case they want to edit it later.
  • Pending Review Status: A campaign will not go live immediately on submisson, but will be reviewed by our community support team. In most cases a campaign will go live within the next 72 hours
  • Vote status: All campaigns that we at [wpv-bloginfo] publish after we've reviewed them thoroughly will be eligible to vote.
  • Donate Status: Only campaigns who reached more than 2000 votes will accept donations
Approval of Pending Reviews

The community support team is careful to inspect each campaign. When the information is verified, the campaign is approved.



More Information

Up-to-date information about a campaign can be found on the campaign page, where the history, background, updates and comments can be viewed. Creators provide youtube thumbnails, quotes and a campaign description on this page.

Anything I should know before contributing?

Difficulties with Contributions

Sometimes a transaction fails, causing problems with a contribution. This can be due to Browser issues, credit card blocks, etc., but a few simple solutions exist. You will know your donation was successful when you are directed to a thank-you page and when you receive an automatic email confirmation.

Common Contribution Errors and their Solutions

Country: If your country is not listed in the drop-down list when trying to donate by credit card, your country may not be supported at this time
Restrictions: We are currently only accepting payments from direct credit/debit cards and via Apple Pay and Paypal

Credit/Debit Card: For the immediate future, if you choose to use a direct credit or debit card, we advise that most offerings accept Visa and MasterCard. Gift carts, pre-paid value cards and debit cards without the Visa or MasterCard logos are not accepted. Product funding in USD may also be paid with American Express; Discover Card may be used for accounts located within the U.S. If you are funding a campaign outside of the US, then only Visa and MasterCard will be accepted.

Apple Pay: Because Apple Pay is supported with huge support from major credit cards and the six largest banks in the U. S., and because it is also accepted in Canada, the UK, Australia and China, it is a preferred method of payment.

Other Payment Options? For other options such as cash, check, PayPal etc., please contact us at [wpv-bloginfo show="admin_email"]

Currency Restrictions: [wpv-bloginfo] is a company based in the U.S., and at the present time, we support only credit and debit payments in in US dollars ($), CAD ($), EUR (€), GBP (£), and AUD ($).

Stripe Restrictions: Payments may be denied if the billing country is different from the bank country.

Contribution Amount Restrictions: Donations and investments are limited to $50,000 per single transaction. It is possible to split contributions into multiple transactions. Please contact *** if you would like to claim a perk of more than $50,000.

Computer Concerns: Make sure to update your Internet Browser to a current version (Internet explorer, Google Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Opera, etc)

Credit Card charges

Reading my Credit Card Statement
When your monthly statement arrives, payments made for projects at [wpv-bloginfo] will list either [wpv-bloginfo] or simply ‘Stripe’. The statement will not show the name of the campaign creator

How Do I Locate Information About the Charge?
Information about the charge can be obtained in two ways:

  • [wpv-bloginfo] sends payment confirmation emails to the email address used by the donor. Please check your inbox, specifically on the date of the charge to locate this email.
  • If you registered a [wpv-bloginfo] account during your last campaign checkout or have an existing one, log in to www.[wpv-bloginfo].com. Click “Account” in the upper right corner. Choose “My donations” from the drop down menu. When you see the donation select ‘View Details’
Unrecognized Credit Card Charges

Please check with your wife or child first, before you assume there was an error. Still don’t recognize the [wpv-bloginfo] account or the campaign? No unauthorized users made the donation? Then please contact our Customer Service Team. A team member will gladly locate the charge. Just be sure to include the following information in your inquiry:

  • Name as it appears on the Credit Card
  • Date of Donation
  • Amount
  • Last four numbers as they appear on the card
  • Name as it appears on the Card
  • Email address associated with this account.
  • With this information, locating the charge should be easy

[wpv-bloginfo] Smile

What is [wpv-bloginfo] Smile?

[wpv-bloginfo] Smile is a fun way to contribute to a Collaboration and support a charitable organization of your choice at the same time. [wpv-bloginfo] will donate 75% of the proceeds which are collected through “[wpv-bloginfo] Smile Mode” to the charitable organization of your choice. You can choose from [postcount] charitable organizations on the checkout page and decide where your funds should go to.

How do I turn on [wpv-bloginfo] Smile?

In order to enable [wpv-bloginfo] Smile scroll to the footer of our Plattform and navigate to the right side. There you’ll see a little switch. 🕹 Just click on it and start smiling.

What collaborations are eligible for charitable donations?

All collaborations are eligible for charitable donations once [wpv-bloginfo] Smile is enabled on your end. You’ll notice an extra step on the checkout page where you can pick one charitable organization or cause of your choice.

Do I have to create a new account when turning on [wpv-bloginfo] Smile?

Turning on [wpv-bloginfo] Smile doesn’t require an account. If you have an existing account with us you are good to go.

How do I choose a charity?
